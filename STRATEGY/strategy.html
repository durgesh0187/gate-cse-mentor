GATE Preparation Strategy

Overview

Preparing for the GATE exam requires a structured approach. This guide provides a step-by-step strategy for effective preparation, emphasizing the importance of lectures, note-making, problem-solving, and previous year questions (PYQs). Following this plan can help you cover 70% of the preparation efficiently.

Preparation Steps

  1. Watch Lectures and Make Notes

    Begin each topic by watching comprehensive lectures. Simultaneously, make detailed notes to aid understanding and retention. Focus on key concepts and formulae.

  2. Daily Practice Problems (DPP)

    After completing the lecture and notes for a topic, solve related Daily Practice Problems (DPP). This helps reinforce the concepts learned and improves problem-solving skills.

  3. Solve Previous Year Questions (PYQs)

    Solve PYQs for each topic after finishing the DPP. Understanding PYQs thoroughly is crucial as it covers around 70% of the preparation. Use the PW PYQ book for an extensive collection of GATE questions.

  4. Topic-wise Tests

    Take topic-wise tests after completing PYQs. These tests help assess your understanding and identify areas that need improvement.

Tips

  • Thorough PYQ Practice: PYQs are essential for GATE preparation. Solve them diligently to understand the pattern and level of questions.
  • Offline Practice: Avoid solving PYQs online. Instead, use a physical book like the PW PYQ book for effective practice.
  • Review and Revise: Regularly review your notes and revise the topics to retain the information.
